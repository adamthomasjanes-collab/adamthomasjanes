# Editorial Copy Audit

## Purpose

This document records the labeling rules used across Issue No. 01 so future additions remain concise, editorial, and non-repetitive.

## Hierarchy rule

Each major chapter may use three distinct layers:

1. **Magenta eyebrow** — adds context or a point of view. It should not repeat the chapter title.
2. **Numbered chapter title** — names the destination clearly.
3. **Running thought** — adds a brief editorial statement or promise.

## Editing rules

- Do not place a second small magenta label directly below a chapter title if it merely repeats that title.
- Use magenta for editorial eyebrows, metadata labels, navigation states, and purposeful accents—not duplicate headings.
- Keep subsection labels only when they add new information, such as *Selected Coursework*, *Role*, *Tools*, or *Outcome*.
- The Colophon owns ghost number **08**. The contact footer is an epilogue and should not carry another ghost 08.
- The site is not offered as an installable web app; no web-manifest link should be added unless that decision changes intentionally.

## Duplicate-Phrase Rule

A phrase may repeat when it performs a different necessary function, such as a project title appearing on both a feature card and its overlay, the core promise appearing as a brand refrain, or issue metadata appearing in both the cover and colophon.

A phrase should be revised when it repeats within the same visual moment without adding hierarchy, context, navigation, or meaning. The Colophon credit and Education introductions were reviewed under this rule in v5.3.1.
